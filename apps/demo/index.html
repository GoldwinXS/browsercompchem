optimize a molecule, then simulate its IR spectrum
simulated absorbance vs wavenumber: harmonic normal modes (same Hessian as above), Lorentzian-broadened, intensity from finite-differenced EHT-Mulliken dipole derivatives (relative 0-100, scaled to the strongest band). Wavenumber decreases left to right, the conventional IR-spectrum axis direction. Symmetry-forbidden modes (e.g. a centrosymmetric molecule's symmetric stretch) plot at essentially zero. Like the orbital energies, this is qualitative: the dipole is a coarse Mulliken point-charge estimate that tends to overestimate polarity (water reads ~2.5 D vs the experimental 1.85 D), so trust the band pattern, the selection-rule zeros, and the dipole's near-zero-by-symmetry — not the absolute Debye value or km/mol scale.
