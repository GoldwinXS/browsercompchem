← back to the app  ·  every "computed" number below was produced by this app's engine (fresh run, 2026-07-23) and re-checked continuously by its test suite. Sources and fuller notes: docs/VALIDATION.md, docs/LITERATURE_VALIDATION.md.

Honest summary: geometries and vibrational frequencies are quantitative (the ML potential reproduces its DFT training target). Orbital energies, IR intensities, and dipoles are qualitative — orderings, degeneracies, selection rules, and band patterns are trustworthy; absolute magnitudes are not, and the app's UI labels this wherever it applies.

4 · IR selection rules (CO2, the hard test)

ModeRel. intensityGroup theory requires
sym. stretch (1413)0.00 — inactiveIR-forbidden (centrosymmetric)
antisym. stretch (2489)100 — strongeststrongly IR-allowed
bend (645, ×2)5.6 eachIR-allowed, degenerate pair

The forbidden mode computes at the numerical noise floor, not just "small." Water: all three modes active (100 / 40.7 / 38.6), as C2v requires. Dipoles: CO2 0.0004 D (zero by symmetry); water computes 2.51 D vs the experimental 1.85 D — Mulliken point charges overestimate polarity, so trust the pattern and the zeros, not absolute intensities.

The ~3.5–4 eV overbinding is EHT's documented systematic offset — note how consistent it is across two very different molecules. That consistency is why orderings, spacings, degeneracies, and shapes are trustworthy while absolute eV are not; the app says so in its UI rather than presenting orbital energies as ionization energies.

6 · Out of scope

  • charged species, radicals, metals, transition states, reactions, solvent (outside ANI-2x's domain)
  • absolute IR intensities and dipole magnitudes (section 4)
  • absolute orbital energies (section 5)
  • certified global-minimum conformer searches (the embedder yields a good untangled local minimum)
